Medicine without Language Barrier's Mission

Imagine that you are getting an ambulatory cataracts removal surgery. In other words, you are completely awake while a person cuts your eye open with scalpel. You start to feel pain and blood running down your cheek. So, you want touch your face; however, when you attempt to do so, all of the people in the room start yelling at you in a language that you do not comprehend and forcefully pin your arms to the bed. How would you feel?

There are approximately 7,105 living languages worldwide. Nevertheless, the majority of the medical doctors in the five continents of the world mainly speak 22 languages. In lingustically diverse countries, physicians' inability to communicate with monolingual patients frequently generate dangerous situations. 'Medicine without Language Barriers' wants to provide physicians and medical personnel with the ability of establishing a basic communication canal with monolingual patients when standard translating services are unavailable. By compiling charts that translate pre-determined phrases phonetically from one language to another through crowdsourcing, the 'Medicine without Language Barriers' team believes that this goal can be accomplished and, therefore, improve the provision of medical services to linguistically isolated communities worldwide.
